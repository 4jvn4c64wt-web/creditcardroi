Still unsure? consider the following tie breakers.
Credit Complexity
The Sapphire Preferred's single $50 hotel credit is straightforward but requires booking through Chase Travel once a year.

The American Express Gold offers up to $424 in annual value through monthly statement credits (Uber Cash, Dining Credit, Dunkin) and a semi-annual Resy credit. Earning this value requires diligence and organically using these partners every month. If you naturally use Uber, Grubhub, and Resy, the Gold card effectively pays for itself. If you forget to use them, the $325 fee becomes a burden.
Travel Partners & Protections
Chase Ultimate Rewards points (CSP) transfer to powerful partners like World of Hyatt and United Airlines. The CSP also comes with primary rental car insurance, a rare and valuable benefit for a mid-tier card. (Note: Chase recently replaced the flat 25% portal point boost with a dynamic "Points Boost" on select bookings.)

Amex Membership Rewards (Gold) transfer to Delta, ANA, and Air Canada Aeroplan, often favoring international premium cabin redemptions. However, the Gold card lacks primary rental car coverage and its trip delay/cancellation insurance is generally less comprehensive than the CSP.
