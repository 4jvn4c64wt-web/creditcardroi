Perk Value and Friction Comparison
The Chase Sapphire Reserve includes a highly flexible $300 Travel Credit that automatically offsets against broad daily commutes, flights, direct hotel stays, tolls, and parking. Its organic value is practically 100% on day one of travel.

Amex Platinum counters with a mathematically superior theoretical credit pool of nearly $3,000. However, these credits are highly fragmented: a $600 Hotel Credit restricted to FHR/HC prepaid portal bookings, a $200 Airline Incidental Credit that excludes actual airfares, and monthly/quarterly distributions (Uber Cash, Resy, Digital Entertainment). Capturing Amex's value requires conforming your spending habits to rigid, non-accumulating monthly and quarterly silos, whereas Chase's offsets are friction-free.
Earning Threshold Break-Even
Under Chase's active card rules, the Sapphire Reserve has discontinued bonuses on generic travel (tolls, parking, subways, and car rentals now yield a baseline 1x). Multipliers are strictly focused on direct flights (4x), direct hotels (4x), Chase Travel portal bookings (8x), and dining (3x).

Amex Platinum remains highly specialized: it earns 5x on flights (booked directly or via portal) and 5x on prepaid hotels booked through Amex Travel. Direct hotel bookings earn only 1x.

The decision often boils down to whether you prefer "frictionless" points on dining and hotel stays (Chase) vs. maximizing high-spend airfare or portal-booking rewards (Amex).
