Card Scenarios

Card Scenarios lets you model what happens if you add, remove, or swap a card in your wallet — using your actual spending data. See how a new card would perform with your real purchases, or find out if dropping a card would cost you more than it saves.

This feature is available to Pro users.

How It Works

The Credit Card Value Tracker calculates the net value of each card in your wallet by comparing what you earn against what you pay.

Net Value = Points Value + Credits Used − Annual Fee

Points Value — Each transaction earns points based on category multipliers (e.g., 3x dining). Points are valued at a per-point rate (cpp).

Credits Used — Statement credits (travel credits, dining credits, etc.) offset the annual fee.

Annual Fee — The yearly cost of holding the card.

A positive net value means the card earns more than it costs. A negative net value means you're paying more than you're getting back.
